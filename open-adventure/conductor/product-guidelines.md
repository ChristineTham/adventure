# Product Guidelines: Open Adventure

## Prose and Voice
- **Classic & Literal:** The game's narrative text, help messages, and documentation should maintain the dry, informative style of the original 1970s game. Avoid overly emotive or modernised descriptions that diverge from the source.
- **Lexicon:** Use Australian English spelling (e.g., 'colour', 'optimise') as per my core instructions.

## UX Principles
- **Accessibility:** Ensure the terminal output is compatible with screen readers and displays correctly across a wide variety of terminal emulators and sizes.
- **Performance:** Maintain the project's legacy of high performance and low resource usage. Commands should execute instantly.
- **Immersion:** Use terminal-based aesthetics and pacing to evoke a sense of late-70s adventure gaming while ensuring modern usability.

## Branding and Visuals
- **Clean & Modern:** While the game is retro, the project's external branding (documentation, icons like `advent.svg`) should use modern typography and clean, high-quality graphics.
- **Visual Consistency:** Ensure any new visual assets align with the existing SVG and PNG assets in the repository.
